Privacy Policy

Last updated March 20, 2026. This Privacy Policy explains how StudyRISC-V collects, uses, stores, and discloses information when you use the landing page, simulator, authentication flows, and saved-program features.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to StudyRISC-V, including the public website, simulator, account sign-in experience, saved-program API, and related documentation. It does not apply to third-party services that may be linked from StudyRISC-V, such as GitHub or Amazon Web Services websites.

2. Information We Collect

StudyRISC-V is designed to be usable without an account for core simulator features. When you choose to sign in or save programs, the service may process the following categories of information:

  • Account information: email address, account identifier, and authentication metadata provided through Amazon Cognito.
  • Saved program content: assembly source code, program names, timestamps, and related metadata you choose to save.
  • Local device data: theme preference, recent simulator history, and other local-only settings stored in your browser.
  • Operational data: standard request, error, and infrastructure logs that may be generated by hosting, API, or security systems.

3. How We Use Information

StudyRISC-V uses information only to operate and improve the service, including to:

  • authenticate users and protect account access;
  • store, retrieve, update, and delete saved assembly programs;
  • determine whether an account qualifies for Georgia Tech Pro features;
  • remember local preferences such as theme selection and local history;
  • monitor reliability, investigate bugs, and prevent abuse; and
  • comply with legal obligations when required.

4. What We Do Not Do

StudyRISC-V does not sell personal information. StudyRISC-V does not use your saved programs or account information for third-party advertising, targeted advertising, or data brokerage. The project is an educational tool, not an ad network or marketing platform.

5. Where Data Is Stored

Account sign-in is handled through Amazon Cognito. Saved programs are stored in Amazon DynamoDB. Depending on how the service is hosted, standard infrastructure components may also store logs or cache data needed to run the product securely and reliably.

Local-only history and theme state are stored in your own browser and are not synced to the cloud unless you explicitly save a program to your account.

6. Legal Bases and User Choices

Where applicable, StudyRISC-V processes personal information based on your consent, the need to provide the service you requested, and legitimate interests in operating a secure educational product. You may stop using the service at any time, avoid signing in, or delete saved programs from your account interface.

7. Data Retention

Saved programs remain associated with your account until you delete them, your account is removed, or the operator removes data for operational, legal, or abuse reasons. Local browser data remains on your device until you clear it or your browser removes it.

8. Sharing and Disclosure

StudyRISC-V may share information only in limited circumstances: with service providers that host the product, when required by law, to enforce the Terms of Use, or to protect the rights, safety, and integrity of the service and its users. Personal information is not shared with data brokers.

9. International and Regional Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or object to certain uses of your personal information. California residents may have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act, including the right to know and the right to request deletion, subject to applicable exceptions. If you are located in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or another region with privacy laws, you may have additional rights under local law.

10. Children’s Privacy

StudyRISC-V is intended for students and other learners, but it is not directed to children under 13. If you believe personal information from a child under 13 has been provided inappropriately, contact the project operator so the issue can be reviewed.

11. Security

StudyRISC-V uses reasonable technical and organizational measures appropriate for a small educational web application, including managed authentication and cloud storage controls. No internet service can guarantee absolute security, so you should avoid storing secrets or highly sensitive personal information in saved programs.

12. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated as StudyRISC-V evolves. Material changes will be reflected by updating the “Last updated” date on this page. Continued use of the service after changes take effect means you accept the revised policy.

13. Contact

Questions or privacy requests may be directed through the StudyRISC-V GitHub repository at github.com/sseth30/riscvsim. Do not post passwords, tokens, or other sensitive information in a public issue.
