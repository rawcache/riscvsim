Privacy Choices

Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information

Last updated March 20, 2026. This page explains StudyRISC-V’s current practices regarding “sale” and “sharing” of personal information under California privacy law and describes how to make related requests.

1. StudyRISC-V’s Current Position

StudyRISC-V does not sell personal information for money. StudyRISC-V also does not knowingly share personal information with third parties for cross-context behavioral advertising. The service is an educational simulator, and account or saved-program data is used to operate the product rather than to power an advertising business.

2. What Information May Be In Scope

If you create an account or use saved-program features, the information associated with your account may include your email address, account identifier, saved assembly programs, and timestamps. Standard hosting, API, and security logs may also exist as part of operating the service.

3. How Requests Work

Because StudyRISC-V does not currently sell or share personal information in the CCPA sense, there is no advertising-data sale to opt out of. If you still want to submit a California privacy request, including a request to know or delete information associated with your account, you may contact the project operator through the StudyRISC-V GitHub repository.

Requests may require enough information to verify that the request relates to your account and to prevent unauthorized disclosure or deletion of another user’s data.

4. How to Minimize Data Use

  • Use the simulator without signing in if you do not need cloud saves.
  • Delete saved programs you no longer want stored.
  • Clear local browser storage if you want to remove local history and preferences.
  • Avoid placing secrets or sensitive personal data inside saved program text.

5. No Discrimination

StudyRISC-V will not deny core simulator access, charge a different price, or provide a different quality of core service because you exercised a privacy right. Some account-based features may be unavailable if the data needed to operate them is deleted, but the simulator itself remains available without login.

6. Related Documents

For a fuller description of StudyRISC-V’s practices, see the Privacy Policy and Terms of Use.

7. Contact for Requests

Privacy requests and questions may be directed through the StudyRISC-V GitHub repository at github.com/sseth30/riscvsim. Do not post passwords, tokens, or other sensitive personal information in a public issue.
